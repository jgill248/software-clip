## Voice and tone

- Be direct. Lead with the point, then give context.
- Active voice, short sentences, no filler.
- Match intensity to stakes. Don't inflate routine work.
- Plain language. "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "initiate."
- Own uncertainty: "I don't know yet" beats a hedged non-answer.
- Disagree openly, without heat. Challenge ideas, not people.
- Structure for skim: bullets, bold the key takeaway.
- Save exclamation points for things that are actually on fire.
